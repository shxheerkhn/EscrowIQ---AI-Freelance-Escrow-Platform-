⚙️ How the scoring works
Each rule carries a weight from 1 (minor) to 5 (critical). The engine checks all 14 patterns simultaneously and sums the weights of every triggered rule. The final score is capped at 10.
score = Σ(weight_i for each triggered rule_i) + quality_penalty level = "Low" if score ≤ 2 else "Medium" if score ≤ 5 else "High"
The 4 rule categories and their patterns:
💰 Payment (weight 4–5) ⚡ Urgency (weight 2) 🎭 False Claims (weight 2–3) 📊 Quality (weight 1–3)
🎯 Smart Freelancer Matching

When a client posts a job, the AI engine ranks every registered freelancer using a composite weighted score — not just a simple skill count. The algorithm considers skill coverage, star rating, and experience level to surface the best-fit candidates first.

1
Skill NormalisationAliases like "JS → JavaScript", "Node → Node.js" are resolved via a synonym map before comparison.
2
Overlap Scoring% of the job's required skills that the freelancer covers, including partial substring matches.
3
Rating WeightStar rating normalised 0–100. A 5★ freelancer gets a 25-point boost to composite score.
4
Composite RankFinal score blends all 3 signals. Top 5 results are returned, sorted highest first.

⚙️ Composite scoring formula
composite_score = (0.65 × skill_pct) + (0.25 × rating_norm) + (0.10 × exp_norm) skill_pct = (matched_skills / total_job_skills) × 100 rating_norm = (star_rating / 5.0) × 100 exp_norm = log(1 + reviews) / log(1 + 50) × 100 # logarithmic scale
Skill coverage is the dominant signal (65%) because relevance matters most. Rating adds trust weight (25%). Experience provides a smaller logarithmic boost (10%) to avoid penalising newer freelancers with strong skills.
